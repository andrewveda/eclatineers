Why Every Engineering Student Should Have a Job

MS
Muhammad Siddhiq J
I BE Cyber Security

I work part-time, and honestly, I think every student should do it too. It's not just about earning money, it's about learning about Life. Many students say that they don't have the time to do all the work given in college but all of us know that that is not true. In many ways, it's been more educational than some of my actual classes. And I think more students (and maybe teachers too) should be talking about that.

"Every hour I work, every step I take, I carry the weight of my dreams and the love of those I hold close"

When I tell people that I am working a part-time job, they assume it is about money. Yes, it is about money but it is much more than that.

It is not about earning, it is also about learning about life. The life lessons I have learnt when I started working are more valuable than the money I have earned so far. The first time I realized how long it takes to earn enough for a night out with friends, it hit me hard that money isn't just numbers on Google Pay. When you work for your income, every rupee has value. You begin budgeting and start calculating how much money and work it takes to earn that.

LEARN LIFE LESSONS, EARN MONEY TOO!

I work in a clothing store. Even though it is run by my relative, and many of my co-workers are my extended family, it is not easy because the expectations are actually higher. I have to constantly earn my place through effort and keep proving my reliability.

This job is physically and mentally demanding. I'm on my feet most of the day, managing stock, assisting customers, and staying alert from morning till night. I started off earning ₹400 a day, and now I earn ₹600 for a full day and ₹350 for a half day. The earnings may seem modest, but the experience is priceless.

There were times when I wasn't fully focused at work. I'd get distracted sometimes by pending college tasks, sometimes just by fatigue. And that's when mistakes happen. I forgot prices, mis-arranged items, or unintentionally ignored a customer. These weren't just small slip-ups. They were wake-up calls. At first, I felt defeated. I even wondered if I was trying to do too much.

But those moments became turning points. They taught me a powerful truth:

"Focus is a form of respect" for the job, for the people around me, and for my own growth.

I started learning from my mistakes, owning them, and improving bit by bit.

STOP TAKING TIME FOR GRANTED

Balancing work and college forces you to actually manage time properly. Instead of spreading an assignment for one whole week and complaining about how difficult it is, we have to try to cram it in the time that we get. There is no time to waste asking "doubts" about the assignments to our friends and then just chatting about something else. Every minute counts. You plan your day with no time to waste - class in the morning, work in the evening, study at night.

Others spend hours scrolling on Instagram and then wondering why they don't have time to do their assignments. They also don't do their assignments and they don't remember the time they spent on Instagram also.

Working part-time has helped me grow so much and has taught me skills and values that syllabus alone could not have taught me.

Key Skills & Values Gained:

  • Time Management
  • Discipline
  • Responsibility
  • Patience
  • Communication
  • Self-Awareness

If you want to learn these skills and values and in addition to that, gain confidence, resilience, grit and different perspectives, you have to start working part-time immediately!

Yes, it's tiring. Yes, you'll make mistakes. But those struggles build character. You will be in a different league, both personally and professionally!

Working has taught me that education doesn't end at the classroom door, it continues through every interaction, every mistake, and every day you choose to grow!

The Ideal Student (According to Society)

CW
Cassandra Maria Wiltshire
I BE Cyber Security

An Ideal Student wakes up at 3.00 a.m and starts the day with the books, aces every test without breaking a sweat, leads every club and volunteers on weekends.

Society loves this fantasy because it keeps you chasing an invisible line. Straight A's, no complaints, dress neatly. That "ideal" student is a myth. It is designed to make you feel less-than if you don't fit the mould.

Being flawless doesn't mean life is never going to hit you. It will. You may fall but you will eventually rise up. But is this practical in our society? No. Because according to society, once you fall, you will never rise.

"Society cheers toppers but forgets the ones who fought battles just to show up."

Our hearts will always crave to hear this one sentence, "You've tried your best." But that will never be the scenario. People won't stop judging. No matter how much you give, there will always be someone expecting more. Be who you are, because that's what matters the most.

But let's be honest. In a system built to reward obedience over originality, it's easier said than done. We're taught to follow, not question. Taught to memorize, not understand, and if you even think about colouring outside the box, you're labelled as a rebel.

We live in a world that demands perfection from students, but barely gives them space to breathe. You're expected to be disciplined like a monk, be productive every minute like machines and be emotionally bulletproof like a superhero.

  • One mistake? You're careless.
  • One low grade? You're slacking off.
  • One breakdown? You're overreacting.

Tired of hearing these things, aren't you?

Some people would preach about mental health but sometimes it's the same people calling you weak behind your back. "Hypocrisy at its peak".

Society claps for toppers but forgets the ones who fought battles just to show up. We're expected to show up, follow the line and blend in, not because it's good for you, but because it's easier to control someone who is failing.

If all these expectations have crushed you, drained you, or made you doubt yourself - then congratulations. Society has made you forget who you really are. But if you've held on, even just a little, protect your peace and fight back in quiet ways…

Then guess what?

You've already won. So stop trying to be flawless. You're human. You're allowed to mess up, take breaks.

"You are not God to be perfect."

AI in the Classroom

AG
Anesh G J
I BE Cyber Security

Take a look at the average Indian classroom. We cant use phones or laptops (AI) in class and if we do, it would be considered 'cheating'. Whether it's a government school in a rural village or a private institution in a metro city, most students are still expected to sit quietly, copy notes, memorize answers, and spit them out in exams.

Now, imagine giving these same students access to an AI that could explain Newton's Laws in Tamil, Malayalam, or English. One that could patiently repeat a concept five times without getting irritated. One that adjusts based on whether you're a visual learner or someone who understands better through examples from cricket or movies.

That's not cheating. That's learning.

"AI doesn't get tired. It doesn't scold. It doesn't have bias."

Students are taught to memorize facts and not learn. But real learning comes from questioning, making mistakes, and figuring things out. AI could be the perfect partner for that journey. It doesn't get tired. It doesn't scold. It doesn't have bias. Whether you're from an English-medium CBSE school or a Government school, AI could provide personalized learning that meets you exactly where you are.

In India, where access to good teachers and quality resources is unequal, AI can be the great leveler. A college student in a rural college could get the same (or better) learning environment that a student in IITs gets. That's revolutionary. Why fear that?

If AI is the future, we should stop treating it like a problem. We should bring it into every classroom. Not just sometimes. Not just for the top students. Everyone should learn how to use it.

The Calculator Analogy

Just like how without calculators, we can't enter Maths classrooms, without AI, we shouldn't be allowed inside all the classrooms. Because in the real world, AI is already being used everywhere. People use it to write emails, make presentations, check grammar, and write code.

Yes, AI has limits. Sometimes it makes mistakes. Sometimes it gives wrong answers. This is why students must learn how to use it wisely. We should learn to double-check what AI says. We should learn to ask good questions.

Right now, many people are scared they will lose their jobs because of AI. But avoiding AI won't help. The best way to deal with it is to learn how to use it.

If we don't learn it in college, when will we learn it?

We are not just preparing for exams.

We are preparing for life.

And in life today, knowing AI is not a choice.

It's a must.

Campus Chronicles: A Gentle Roast

MN
Mathesh N
I BE Cyber Security

As a Cyber Security student, I am training to scan systems for patterns, vulnerabilities, and unusual behaviour. Every department has its own patterns not just in their syllabus, but in the way students talk, chill, and even panic before exams!

These light-hearted observations of my fellow first year students aren't meant to offend. They're only notes from the Cyber Security system part of the system defending the entire system! It is written with humour, curiosity, and a lot of affection. After all, if we can debug code, we can debug culture too.

CSE

This class has the energy that everyone thinks they're the next Elon Musk, but don't even know why some websites won't open. If it doesn't load, they just say: "Server down dude." But hey future Elon Musks might be getting training here.

IT

If CSE is the elder brother suffering in silence, then IT is the chill younger sibling living life on easy mode. They enjoy more freedom, less stress, and a syllabus that's 80% copy-paste from CSE but with all the complex stuff conveniently omitted. Still top in the exams. Future Elon Musks' siblings might be getting training here.

ECE

Forever confused between circuits. Can't even draw a diagram to connect a basic night lamp. They think they can fix any electronic device but don't know the difference between a resistor and resistivity. Still, they're the creative problem solvers who somehow make those confusing circuits actually work.

EIE

No one knows what they actually do. Not even them. So I also don't know. But I think they are secretly solving all the World's problems.

EEE

Professional wire inspectors who can't even explain how a transformer works. They'll shout, "Don't touch that da, live wire!" Bruh, that wire isn't even plugged into the circuit. Every lab ends with more internal shock than electrical shock. But when it comes to real work, they're the backbone of every campus project, giving excellent idEEEas!

MECHANICAL

99% guys? Hell feels like heaven compared to their classroom. Yet these warriors proudly roar, "Mech dhaan da gethu!" and bring roaring machines to life. Hats off!

CIVIL

They say, "I'll build your house one day." Bruh, first draw the EG diagram properly. But credit where it's due they're the ones who turn blueprints into reality, brick by brick.

AGRI

The Agri gang? Forever explaining that yes, it's a real engineering course. They'll say, "Soil la strength iruku, but semester la illa." But behind the jokes, they're the ones making farming smarter, easier, and more sustainable.

AIDS

These folks think they're building the next Jarvis, Iron Man's AI assistant, no less. They spend half their time Googling "How to create AI like Jarvis" and the other half asking ChatGPT the same question. And ChatGPT? It's like, "Who the hell are you guys?" But hey, their hype is real they'll be the ones probably running campus one day with their AI overlords.

MDE

These guys are the tech doctors on campus. If your device acts weird, they'll switch it off and on. Do you think they know how an oscilloscope works? Still, when campus tech needs a cure, they're the real heroes in the lab coat.

CYBER SECURITY

My department at last! We are expected to wear hoodies, dark shades, and carry a laptop like we're about to hack NASA. Whenever we open a website, we imagine "Access Granted" flashing in green font with numbers raining down Matrix-style.

Reality? 99% of the time we're Googling "What is Cyber Security?"

Laptop? Dead. Internet? Gone. Confidence? 404 Error.

Still, we'll warn you "Bro, don't click that link, it's phishing!"

Like, bruh, we can't even open a Zoom link without technical issues.

But hey, one day, we would be the Guardians of the Cyber Galaxy. Legends in the making.

"No matter which department you belong to, just know that your branch has its own spark, its own quirks, and its own kind of brilliance."

Conclusion: Some of us code, some connect circuits, some work with soil and concrete, and all of us work with AI. And together, we make this crazy, chaotic, and unforgettable college universe.

SRM Valliammai - Many Minds and one heart for an amazing future!

The Light Within the Shadows

When I stood before this sky, Where day slowly surrendered to night, I saw something more than just a sunset. The rays of light, Piercing through the dim horizon, Reminded me of life itself - How even in moments of darkness, There's a direction, A purpose.

"But the light? Oh the light kissed every corner like it had waited just for me"

Sometimes, I feel lost, Like the shadows stretching quietly across the earth below But then I realize Those shadows exist Only because something within me still shines.

Even when my world feels quiet, Still, uncertain… There's a silent strength rising in me, Like those soft beams reaching into the unknown.

The fading orange glow reminds me: endings aren't failures. They are transitions. The most beautiful changes Don't happen in the brightness of day, But in the stillness of transformation.

And the sky whispered to me: You don't need to be perfect or bright all the time. You just need to keep rising, Even in pieces, Just like the light does - one soft ray at a time.

The Butterfly in the Storm

I once believed I needed comfort zones To feel like I could grow Soft people, Safe days, Smooth paths. I thought I needed peace To even begin blooming.

Then I saw it. A butterfly. And In that one moment, I understood something I hadn't known before.

I'm not here To grow only when Everything's perfect. I'm built for the storm!

My softness Is not weakness. It is my power. I don't just survive I rise. And I rise Gently, But surely, Like fireflies Dancing in the dark.

Homecoming

I've walked this road a hundred times Feet moving, eyes open, But soul elsewhere. And yet today Something shifted.

The pavement didn't change. The buildings didn't move. But the light? Oh, the light kissed every corner Like it had waited just for me.

The silence didn't feel empty it felt full, like it had been holding its breath, waiting for me to finally notice it.

Maybe It was never the world That was too fast. Maybe it was me Rushing Chasing Surviving.

I was so busy Trying to reach The next version of myself, I forgot how to be The one I already am.

Today, I didn't walk to escape. I walked to feel.

And every crack in the road, Every flicker of sunset on the windows, whispered to me "You've always been here. But today… You've arrived."

There is holy magic in the ordinary. In the forgotten streets, The soft light, The gentle air That doesn't ask me to do anything But just exist.

I am not racing to become. I am remembering How to just be. And that, in itself, Is a kind of homecoming.

When the World Went Quiet, I Heard Myself

I used to think Peace was something I had to chase Loud laughs, Crowded rooms, Constant noise.

But today, I found it Right here. Before the first drop of rain. Before the thunder woke the sky.

Just me, The trees, The ancient air, And a kind of stillness so quiet it almost felt like time stopped breathing.

And in that breathless pause, I heard something clearer than any prayer. Stillness is not emptiness. It's awareness. It's where my overthinking mind Finally slows, and my soul softly answers, "I'm here. I've always been here."

This place didn't ask me to speak. It didn't ask me to perform, to explain, to shine. It just let me exist.

And maybe that's the lesson That the most sacred things in life don't need to announce themselves. They don't shout. They simply are.

And when I finally stop running, I see them. I am not lost. I am just learning How to sit in the silence Where peace has been Waiting for me All along.

I Can Dream Big and Still Be Grounded

As I stand here and look out at this huge view, Giant buildings stretching so high, Strong, green trees below a soft, grey sky, I feel a very old and deep knowing grow in my heart.

It's like I'm seeing the whole big story of life Laid out in front of me, in one quiet moment.

Here are the dreams of people, Built up carefully, brick by brick, Trying to reach the very clouds, Almost as if they want to touch or hold the sky.

But then, right there too, are the trees Ancient and wise, holding their ground, Roots deep, deep in the earth. Their yellow flowers like thousands of tiny, happy stars Scattered all over the ground, bringing joy.

This whole picture, this entire scene, Tells me a profound truth: Life is a perfect, beautiful mix of everything.

It's about the big plans we make, The huge things we try to build with our hands and minds, And also the deep, quiet, natural world That just is - without trying to be anything else.

I feel a constant pull inside me, A gentle tug-of-war. One part wants more, To do great things, To achieve big goals. The other part just wants to be, To simply exist, To feel peace in the moment.

This view reminds me, with deep, comforting peace, That I don't have to pick only one way to live.

I can build my biggest, most amazing dreams The ones that reach really high. But at the same time, I must always find my roots, Just like these wise trees dig deep into the earth.

I can live in a busy world, Full of noise and movement, But I must always find my quiet moments Those peaceful times, Like the soft, calm light resting under this grey sky.

This whole view teaches me, truly and deeply, That real strength isn't only in what I do, Or what I build for the world to see.

It comes from living with all these different parts of life The busy and the quiet, The building and the growing.

It comes from finding my own deep, silent peace, In the perfect, calm balance Between what I create with my efforts And what simply grows around me Always there, Always being.

This is a peace that stays with me, No matter what.

The Quiet Strength of Growth Unseen

When I look very, very closely At these small, green buds, All closed up And quietly waiting to open, A powerful and kind truth fills my heart.

These are not flowers yet. Not fully open, Not bright for everyone to see. Instead, they are small, perfect promises, Held tight inside.

They remind me of myself On days when I feel small, When I feel like I'm not growing fast enough, When I compare myself to others Who seem to be blooming already.

But these buds teach me something beautiful: Growth doesn't have to be loud. Growth doesn't have to be fast. Growth doesn't have to be seen by everyone to be real.

There is quiet strength In the waiting. There is deep power In the preparation. There is sacred magic In the unseen becoming.

Just like these buds, I am growing Even when no one is watching. Even when I can't see it myself. Even when it feels like nothing is happening.

My growth is happening In the silence of my heart, In the depth of my thoughts, In the roots of my being That are reaching deeper Into the soil of my experiences.

I don't need to rush my blooming. I don't need to force my opening. I just need to trust The process That is already working Inside me.

And when the time is right, When the conditions are perfect, When I have grown enough In the quiet, unseen spaces…

I will bloom. Beautifully. Naturally. In my own unique way.

Until then, I honor this quiet time. I celebrate this unseen growth. I trust this sacred process.

Because even the most magnificent flowers Started exactly like this: Small, closed, Quietly becoming.
